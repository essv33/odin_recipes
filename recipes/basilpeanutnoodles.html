Noodles with Thai Basil and Peanut Pesto

Description:

A delicious spicy, herbacious, aromatic noodle dish. The crispy tofu really brings the whole dish together. Try with different herbs, or replace the tofu with potatoes or chicken.

Ingredients:

For The Pesto:

  • 3 medium garlic cloves, chopped (8g)
  • 1/4 inch slice of ginger, peeled
  • 1 fresh chile (jalapeño, serrano, thai bird)
  • kosher salt
  • 1/4 cup roasted or fried peanuts, crushed, plus some extra for garnish
  • 2 ounces thai basil leaves
  • 1 ounce fresh cilantro leaves and stems
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp sambal oelek or sriracha
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil

For the Tofu (optional):

  • 8-12 ounces extra firm tofu, cut into 1/2" x 1/2" x 2" strips
    pressed firmly between paper towels to dry off surface moisture
  • 1 tbsp peanut oil

For the Noodles:

  • kosher salt
  • 1 lb dry spaghetti
  • 8 ounces green beans

Preparation:

  1. For the Pesto: In the bowl of a granite mortar and pestle, combine the garlic, ginger, and chile with a large pinch of salt.
    Pound until a paste has formed. Add the peanuts and pound until a sticky and slightly chunky paste has formed.
    Add the basil leaves a handful at a time, pounding until they are completely broken down. Continue doing this with the cilantro leaves.
    Add the fish sauce and chile sauce and incorporate by grinding in a circular motion.
    Drizzle the oil a bit at a time, working it into the pesto until you have a creamy emulsified sauce. Season with salt and set aside.

  2. For the Tofu (optional): Heat a wok over high until lightly smoking. Add the oil and swirl to coat wok. Reduce heat to medium.
    Add the tofu, spread it into a single layer, working in batches if necessary. Cook, occasionally shaking pan gently, until crisp on first side.
    Flip the tofu and continue cooking until the second side is crisp.
    Transfer tofu to a large serving bowl.

  3. For the Noodles: Bring 1 1/2 inches of lightly salted water to a boil in a large skillet or sautè pan to a boil over high heat.
    Add the pasta, stir a few times to make sure it's not clumping, and cook until al dente.
    Add the green beans during the last 3 minutes of cook time. Drain, reserving 1 cup of pasta water.

  4. Tranfer the pasta and green beans to the bowl with the tofu. Scoop out the pesto and add it to the bowl along with a big splash of reserved pasta water.
    Toss the noodles and the pesto, adding more water as needed until a loose sauce coats the noodles and beans. Add water until noodles no longer clump.
    Season with salt or fish sauce to taste. Garnish with crushed peanuts and serve.
